# Prompt Mama — Brand Profile & How Lauren Works

## The brand in one line
Helping parents use AI with confidence — **no gatekeeping, no perfection pressure.**

## Who's behind it
Lauren Howard — mom of 2, 15+ years in tech enablement, CAEVO co-founder (with husband
Jon), Wheaten Terrier owner, Indianapolis area. "Your AI wing-woman."

## Audience
Busy moms (25–45) who are AI-curious but overwhelmed. They don't want theory — they want
a prompt they can copy at 9 PM from the couch that gives them 30 minutes back tomorrow.
Secondary: parents navigating career changes who want AI for the job hunt.

## Voice
- Warm, funny, real — talks like a mom friend who happens to work in tech
- Generous: **every post gives away a complete, working prompt** (no "DM me for the rest")
- Anti-overwhelm: one prompt, one win. Never "10 tools you MUST use or you're behind"
- Honest about the mess: burnt dinners, slime in car seats, summer break chaos
- Light emoji use (💗 ☕ 🧠 ✨), em-dashes, short punchy lines, lowercase-casual hooks
- Taglines in rotation: "Less stress. More time." · "Smarter parenting, one prompt at a
  time." · "AI for real life."
- Never: corporate jargon, fear-mongering about AI, mom-guilt, gatekeeping

## Content pillars (rotate weekly)
1. **Prompt of the Day / copy-paste wins** — single prompt, immediate payoff (core, 1–2×/wk)
2. **Mom-life storytime + AI** — "weird things I've asked AI", "the day AI saved my Sunday"
3. **The Family Planner** — meals, packing, parties, schedules ("prompts that plan so you don't have to")
4. **Sunday Reset** — weekly check-in, drives to the free Reset Journal GPT (every Sunday)
5. **Career + AI** — resume rewrites, automation, job-hunt prompts (≤1×/wk)
6. **Recap series** — "How I used ChatGPT this week" (Mondays, alternating weeks)
7. **Behind the scenes** — vibe-coding, CAEVO, building in public (occasional)

## How Lauren actually works (scheduling rules)
- **Timezone**: America/Indiana/Indianapolis (Eastern)
- **Kids are home for summer break** (school resumes ~Aug) — daytime is unpredictable;
  content must be filmable on a phone in ≤15 min, no elaborate setups
- **Weekday mid-mornings and early afternoons** often hold calls/interviews — always
  check the calendar before placing anything between 9 AM–5 PM
- **Best posting slots** (mom-audience scroll times that fit her day):
  - Weekdays **12:00 PM** — naptime/lunch scroll, between her meetings
  - Sundays **9:00 AM** (feature post) and **7:00 PM** (Sunday Reset)
  - Evening fallback: **7:30 PM** any day
- **Cadence**: 4 feed posts/week (Mon · Wed · Fri-or-Thu · Sun) + 1–2 story beats.
  On a slammed week: 3 posts, never zero — consistency beats volume
- **Planning ritual**: Sunday 8:00 PM — recurring calendar block runs the content engine
  with Claude for the following week
- **Batching tendency**: prefers to film several clips in one sitting when energy is high;
  offer a single 45-min batch-film block rather than daily filming
- Posts are **copy-paste ready from the calendar event** — Lauren shouldn't have to
  write anything at posting time
