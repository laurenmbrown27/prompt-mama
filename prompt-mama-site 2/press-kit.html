Lauren Howard is a tech enablement leader, AI educator, and entrepreneur known for making artificial intelligence approachable for the people who need it most: busy parents and the caregivers quietly holding families together.

After 15+ years inside big tech, Lauren saw firsthand how transformative AI was becoming for businesses — and how few of those tools were reaching real households. She founded Prompt Mama as a passion project, sharing copy-paste prompts and practical AI shortcuts for moms juggling work, family, and the mental load. The brand grew from zero to a thriving Instagram community of 3,500+ engaged followers, with content reaching 50,000+ views in a single 90-day window and 61% of those views coming from non-followers — a sign her work travels.

In 2026, Lauren co-founded CAEVO with her husband Jon — an AI-powered platform reimagining how families age, care, and plan for what's next. The platform addresses a hidden crisis: 63 million Americans serve as unpaid family caregivers, contributing $1 trillion in unpaid labor each year, with 70% receiving no preparation before caregiving begins.

Lauren brings a rare combination of enterprise tech credibility, real-mom relatability, and the storytelling instincts to make AI feel useful instead of intimidating. Her work sits at the intersection of women in tech, family-centered AI, and what she calls "AI for real life." She lives in Indianapolis with her husband, two kids, and a Wheaten Terrier named Harry.

The audience

Who's actually listening — and why brands & outlets find this interesting.

Who Lauren reaches

The Prompt Mama audience is a tightly-defined community of millennial and Gen X moms — most working full- or part-time, with school-age kids at home. They're tech-curious but time-poor: looking for tools that fit into 5-minute windows, not 60-minute tutorials.

It's an audience advertisers struggle to reach because it's underserved by both mom content (often anti-tech) and tech content (rarely relatable). Lauren sits in the middle.

Why content travels

61% of recent views came from non-followers — meaning content is being saved, shared, and surfaced via Reels recommendations rather than relying on follower base alone. Educational carousels regularly hit 10–25K views per post.
